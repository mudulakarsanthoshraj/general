The Ask

We’d like approval for a single, low-cost API key so our team can use an approved AI model (we suggest GPT-4o Mini) inside our own apps, on our own server. Today our network rules block this, so even a small, safe test isn’t possible.

What we’re asking management to approve

  1. One approved API key to one low-cost AI model (GPT-4o Mini, or a similar approved model), used only on our server.
  2. A small monthly spending limit on that key — around $25–$50, set by leadership. Expected real spend is about $1–$30 a month; the limit is a safety ceiling, not a fixed bill.
  3. A Zscaler exception for that one web address, through the normal Security approval steps.

What this is

  • One approved model, one key, used on our server.
  • Small, watched usage for learning and trying ideas.
  • A reusable setup that future approved ideas can share.

What this is not

  • Not a key for every engineer or laptop.
  • Not Copilot, ChatGPT, or Claude — those are separate decisions.
  • Not a change to Zscaler or any company-wide network rule.
In one line
For a small, capped monthly cost — with no private data shared and all security rules followed — we get hands-on access to use AI safely inside our own apps.

Why It Helps

What changes for the company and the team once we can use AI safely in our own apps.

Today, without access

  • We can’t try real AI ideas while the network blocks access.
  • We can’t check the cost or quality of AI before committing to build.
  • The team falls behind on real AI skills and good adoption choices.

With safe access

  • The team can try real AI ideas directly in our apps.
  • We get real usage data to make smart, low-cost AI choices.
  • We spot ways to automate and save time before spending big.
  • The team keeps learning, in a safe and approved way.
Bottom line
A small, safe start lets us use AI the smart way — learn first, see what works, then grow — instead of guessing from theory or vendor demos.

What We’d Try First

One small, low-risk example so it’s clear what the key is actually for.

A good first test is a plain-language helper inside an app we already have. Instead of filling a long form, the user types what they want in everyday words — for example, “Book the cheapest flight to Canada on 15 June, meals included, any time is fine.” The AI reads that sentence and turns it into the neat, structured fields our app already understands.

The user sees those details, checks them, and clicks Confirm — and only then is anything saved. The AI just reads and organizes the text; the person always makes the final decision, and nothing is stored without confirmation.

Why this is a good first test
  • Useful: faster for users, with fewer forms and fewer drop-offs.
  • Cheap: a request like this costs well under a tenth of a cent.
  • Safe: public data only, runs on our server, and the user confirms before anything is saved.

What It Costs

AI models charge per “token” (about ¾ of a word) for the text sent in and the answer sent back. GPT-4o Mini is one of the cheapest good models.

Model Best for Input / 1M Output / 1M
GPT-4o mini (recommended) Cheap, fast answers, chatbots, organizing data $0.15 $0.60
GPT-4.1 mini A small quality step up, if needed $0.40 $1.60
GPT-4o Higher quality; handles text, images, audio $2.50 $10.00

Prices are per 1 million tokens, shown for context. Check current rates at openai.com/api/pricing before purchase.

What this means in real money
A typical request on GPT-4o Mini costs well under a cent. Even at 10,000 requests a month that’s about $7 — far below the proposed limit. That’s why we expect real spend of just $1–$30 a month, with the cap as a safety ceiling.
Right tool for the job
Low-cost models like GPT-4o Mini are great for chatbots, finding and organizing data, and summarizing — but not for writing complex code or hard problem-solving. We use the cheap model for everyday tasks, and only consider a pricier model if a specific need comes up.

How It Stays Safe

Simple rules that keep the spend, the data, and the answers under control.

Rule How it works
One approved model We start with GPT-4o Mini only. Any new model goes through Security review.
Monthly spending limit The key has a fixed monthly cap and stops on its own when it’s reached.
No private data Public docs and sample data only — no personal details (names, emails, employee IDs), no private code, no passwords.
Runs only on our server The key and logic stay on our server, never in the browser or on a laptop.
Not used for training We use the vendor’s “no-training” tier, so our prompts aren’t kept.
Full record Every prompt and response is logged for 30 days so Security can review it.
Honest answers Answers come from approved data; the model says “I don’t know” when unsure, and the team checks a sample regularly.
Sign-off per use case A short one-page plan (owner, data used, spending limit, success measure) is approved by Security, Platform, and the product owner before going live.
